# Chapter 1 Structure: The Invisible Container

## Scene Breakdown

### Scene 1: Dawn at Limhamn Harbor (600 words)
- Sara arrives at harbor, 6 AM, winter darkness
- Meet dockworker Petri who called it in
- Walk to the container in question
- Establish: port atmosphere, cold, industrial, automated systems visible
- Key moment: "It's been here six weeks, but also... it hasn't."

### Scene 2: The Ghost Container (700 words)
- Examining the container exterior - nothing unusual physically
- Petri explains: GPS shows it moving every few nights
- Port logs show regular transfers between zones
- But no human operator logged any moves
- Crane system says it moved the container, but no one told it to
- Key moment: Opening the container - just a phone

### Scene 3: Emil Arrives (500 words)
- Introduce Emil - IT forensics
- His dynamic with Sara - friendly, collaborative
- Initial analysis of the phone
- Key moment: "It's pretending to be cargo."

### Scene 4: The Explanation (800 words)
- Emil explains adversarial ML attacks
- Phone running fake IoT signatures
- Temperature sensors, weight distributions, refrigeration
- Container looked "normal" to the AI
- Sara's growing understanding and dread
- Key moment: "How many more?"

### Scene 5: The Discovery (400 words)
- Checking port records with new understanding
- 30 containers with similar patterns
- All empty now
- The real smuggling happened months ago
- Key moment: Sara realizes they're chasing ghosts - they already lost

## Pacing Notes
- Start with sensory detail and atmosphere
- Build mystery gradually - what's wrong with the container?
- Technical explanation in middle - let Emil teach Sara (and reader)
- End on escalation and dread - this is bigger than one container

## Emotional Arc
- Sara: Professional competence → curiosity → understanding → dread → determination
- Emil: Mild cynicism → teacher mode → darker cynicism about being too late

## Key Dialogue Beats
- Petri's confusion about the container being "there but not there"
- Sara admitting she doesn't understand the tech
- Emil's patient explanation with analogies
- Sara's final line about what this means for everything else

## World Building Elements
- Automated port systems - cranes, logistics, container management
- Winter darkness in Malmö
- Mix of old dockworkers and new automation
- Swedish police procedures and professional culture
