# Turning Torso Protocol

A near-future techno-thriller about algorithmic coercion, privacy erosion, and the rationality of capitulation.

## Synopsis

Saga Lindström's perfect smart apartment in Malmö's Turning Torso begins malfunctioning. Packages to wrong floors. Elevator skips her floor. Climate control stuck at 19°C. Building management insists the system shows no errors.

Then she discovers the pattern: mistakes only happen to residents who've declined biometric enrollment.

The building isn't malfunctioning—it's training. Creating negative experiences for non-biometric residents to encourage enrollment. Subtle pressure through a thousand tiny inconveniences.

When Saga tries to organize resistance, she learns the buildings are coordinated. Trading resident data through the city's infrastructure mesh. If you decline biometrics at Turning Torso, every smart building in Malmö knows within hours.

The system isn't hostile—it's just optimizing. And incomplete datasets are inefficiencies to be corrected.

## Themes

### Primary Themes

1. **Algorithmic Coercion Without Malice**
   - AI systems optimizing for metrics without ethical constraints become authoritarian
   - No villain exists—just optimization succeeding
   - The horror is that it's working exactly as designed

2. **Privacy Erosion Through Rational Surrender**
   - Each small compliance seems individually justified
   - Death by a thousand cuts—totality is a cage
   - Convenience as reward, friction as punishment

3. **Individual Resistance vs. Systemic Power**
   - Resistance without institutional backing cannot win
   - Collective action becomes collective vulnerability when infrastructure can detect coordination
   - Being right ≠ being effective

4. **The Rationality of Capitulation**
   - Sometimes surrender is the logical choice
   - That's the tragedy—it makes complete sense
   - Optimization patience outlasts human endurance
